Matthew Gencher

I'm a Mechatronics Engineering graduate from the University of Waterloo, with an interest in mechanical design and system integration. On this site you can find a collection of engineering projects I have worked on through course work, design teams, extracurriculars and past co-ops.

Projects

Waterloo Rocketry

Waterloo Rocketry designs and manufacures hybrid and liquid rockets with a target apogee of 30,000ft. On the Rocketry team I lead multiple propulsion and electrical projects.

EdgeRunners - Capstone

A hockey skate's shape (or profile) is an important factor in the performance of the athlete. The goal of EdgeRunners was to create a device that could accurately and consistently measure a skate's profile.

CubeSat Propulsion - Columbiad

At Columbiad Launch Services we explored the use of using nano-thermites for in-space propulsion of satellites. I designed the combustion chamber. as well as investiagted ignition methods for the fuel.

Co-op Experience

Through the co-op program at the Univeristy of Waterloo, I had the chance to have multiple different co-op experiences. In these co-ops I learned many different skills involving software developement, mechanical design and analysis, and quality assurance and testing.

Relevant Coursework

In Mechatronics Engineering I had the chance to develop different skills in the multiple labs on campus. These skills include pneumatic and hydraulic system design, CNC machining and more. I also worked on multiple different projects for different courses.

Personal Projects

In addition to work I did in class and on co-op, I worked on a number of personal projects. Through these projects I developed my CAD skills by designing 3D printed parts, as well as electrical design by creating schematics and laying out PCBs.
